The Problem

Jobs exist across communities, but they often collapse when cash is scarce or digital payments are mistrusted. Work is delayed or abandoned — not because labor or demand is missing, but because the exchange of value breaks down.

The Solution

How Kuza Ledger works

01
Onboarding
Trust-Based Entry

Workers and employers are verified by trusted community agents using local recognition and social proof — no cash or digital payment required.

02
Execution
Work Happens

Jobs and services are carried out as agreed, even when immediate payment is unavailable. The system doesn't pause for liquidity.

03
Value Capture
Work Credits Issued

Completed work is recorded as credits (IOUs) in a community trust ledger, capturing value instead of losing it.

04
Governance
Risk Containment

Credit limits, time-bound credits, and transparent oversight prevent abuse and runaway debt within the community.

05
Resolution
Anchored Settlement

Pre-identified buyers, SMEs, cooperatives, or institutions periodically settle accumulated credits with cash once liquidity becomes available.

06
Outcome
Workers Get Paid

Workers receive payment, employers keep operating, and jobs continue even during cash shortages. The community economic cycle holds.

Liquidity Resolution

How value converts to cash

Kuza Ledger does not assume cash is available at the moment of work. Value is recorded and pooled, then converted into cash through anchored settlement partners during scheduled settlement cycles.

The Tradeoff

Trust risk for job continuity

This system accepts higher trust management and verification risk in exchange for job continuity when conventional payment systems fail. The community is the collateral.
